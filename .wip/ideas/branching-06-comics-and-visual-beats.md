# Comics and visual beats in branching fiction

## Problem

The motivating Elsewhere idea is not just prose with links. It is inspired partly by the spirit of Land of Lisp: playful, strange, programming-adjacent, and open to blending text with comics or drawings. This is not educational content, but the visual/comic energy matters.

If the framework only models prose scenes, it may miss one of the most distinctive possibilities of the project.

## Solution

Explore how branching fiction can include visual beats: comic panels, drawings, recurring visual jokes, diagrams, fake interfaces, marginalia, clue images, or scene illustrations. Visuals could be attached to graph nodes, choices, endings, or transitions.

This visual storytelling layer is distinct from the authoring graph. The graph helps the author and agent build the story; comics and drawings are part of what the reader may experience in Elsewhere.

The authoring workflow should be able to mark where a visual beat would improve the story, even if the final art is hand-drawn, generated elsewhere, or left as a placeholder during drafting.

## Requirements

- Allow story nodes or choices to reference optional visual assets or visual prompts.
- Support placeholder visual beats during writing.
- Consider pacing: when an image carries a joke, clue, reveal, or pause better than prose.
- Keep the first version lightweight enough that the visual layer does not block story authoring.
- Preserve the weird/playful inspiration without turning the project into a tutorial.
