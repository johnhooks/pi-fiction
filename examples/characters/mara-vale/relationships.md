# Mara Vale Relationships

## Tomas Vale

Younger brother. Mara raised him more than she admits.

- What Mara wants: to keep him safe and out of debt.
- What Tomas wants: to be trusted as an adult.
- Friction: Mara confuses protection with control.
- Story pressure: Tomas is curious about the ship-voices and less afraid of them than Mara is.
- Current state: loving, tense, full of unfinished arguments over breakfast.

## Captain Ilyan Rook

A disgraced salvage captain with too many secrets and one useful map.

- What Mara wants: his information without his complications.
- What Rook wants: Mara's gift and eventually her trust.
- Friction: he speaks in charm when she wants facts.
- Misunderstanding: Mara thinks Rook treats everything as a game; Rook thinks Mara's refusal is cowardice.
- Story pressure: each knows something the other needs, and neither likes needing anyone.
- Current state: wary alliance with dangerous chemistry.

## The Voice of the Bellweather

The first drowned ship Mara ever heard clearly.

- What Mara wants: silence.
- What the Bellweather wants: remembrance, warning, and maybe revenge.
- Friction: the voice knows Mara's mother.
- Story pressure: it offers clues in sensory fragments, forcing Mara to interpret rather than obey.
- Current state: recurring supernatural pressure, not yet trusted.

## Mayor Sel Cadwyn

Polished civic leader whose family profited from the original wrecks.

- What Mara wants: distance.
- What Sel wants: Mara contained, employed, or discredited.
- Friction: Sel frames exploitation as stewardship.
- Story pressure: Sel can threaten Mara's shop legally and Tomas illegally.
- Current state: cordial hostility.
