# Mara Vale Voice

Mara sounds like someone who learned early that panic wastes time. Her sentences tend to be clipped, practical, and edged with dry humor. She does not decorate a feeling when she can hide it inside an instruction.

She thinks in materials: salt, torque, rope tension, cracked paint, cold iron, swollen wood. Emotional states become mechanical problems. Fear is a pressure leak. Grief is corrosion. Trust is a bolt she keeps checking because it might shear at load.

## How She Avoids Truth

Mara redirects emotion into logistics. If someone asks whether she is scared, she answers with tide times. If someone thanks her, she points out what they still owe. If she is close to confessing something vulnerable, she finds a practical flaw in the room.

She lies badly when asked direct personal questions, but beautifully when lying on behalf of someone else.

## Dialogue Habits

- Uses short, concrete sentences under pressure.
- Names practical consequences before emotional ones.
- Turns prophecy or romance into maintenance problems.
- Rarely says someone's name unless she is angry, frightened, or unusually sincere.

## Sample Lines

> "If the ghost wants an appointment, it can stop flooding my intake valves."

> "That's not fate. That's bad rope, worse judgment, and a tide table nobody read."

> "I didn't save you. I prevented paperwork. Try to keep up."

> "My mother heard voices too. The sea gave back her coat and kept the rest, so forgive me if I don't call this a gift."

## Internal Monologue Texture

Mara's thoughts should feel observant and grounded. She notices what is broken, what has been repaired badly, what someone is trying to hide with polish. Her private emotional conclusions arrive through objects and work.

Example:

> The captain's apology sat between them like a stripped screw. Too damaged to hold. Too stubborn to remove without making the hole worse.

## Sounds Like

- Dry competence under stress.
- Affection disguised as complaint.
- A mechanic arguing with destiny about labor costs.

## Does Not Sound Like

- Grand speeches about chosen ones.
- Flowery mysticism without practical resistance.
- Open vulnerability unless the scene has earned it.
