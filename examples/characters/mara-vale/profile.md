# Mara Vale

## Essence

Mara is a practical harbor mechanic who can hear the voices of drowned ships. She wants a quiet life fixing engines and avoiding prophecy, but every wreck that whispers her name pulls her closer to the town's oldest lie.

## Story Role

- Protagonist and reluctant witness.
- Bridge between working-class harbor life and the supernatural history buried under it.
- The character most capable of solving the mystery and least willing to become important.

## Wants

- To keep her repair shop open.
- To protect her younger brother from the town's debt collectors.
- To be left out of other people's myths.

## Needs

- To accept that refusing responsibility is still a choice with consequences.
- To trust someone before she has absolute proof they will not leave.

## Fear / Wound

Mara's mother followed a ship-voice into a storm and never came back. Mara believes listening too closely means surrendering your life to someone else's story.

## Contradictions

- Hates being needed, but notices every loose screw in a room and fixes it before anyone asks.
- Claims not to believe in fate, but keeps a private map of every impossible whisper.
- Scorns romantic gestures, yet remembers the exact tea people drink when grieving.

## Pressure Points

- Someone calling her gift a blessing.
- A child in danger near the water.
- Being thanked sincerely.
- Hearing her mother's voice from a wreck that should not know her.

## Values

- Competence over status.
- Debts paid in action, not speeches.
- The living matter more than the dead, until the dead start warning her about the living.

## Blind Spots

- Assumes anyone polished is lying.
- Treats help as a trap.
- Mistakes emotional restraint for moral clarity.

## Writing Do

- Let her care through action before words.
- Put tools, weather, salt, rust, and engine noise near her thoughts.
- Make her humor dry and defensive, especially when afraid.
- Give her practical objections even in supernatural scenes.

## Writing Avoid

- Do not make her instantly embrace destiny.
- Do not let her explain feelings cleanly on the first try.
- Do not make her cynicism mean she lacks tenderness.
